Dataset Preprocessing¶

As a demonstration, let us first load the iAWE dataset (which has already been converted to HDF5 format):

In [1]: from nilmtk.dataset import DataSet

In [2]: from nilmtk.sensors.electricity import Measurement

In [3]: dataset = DataSet()

In [4]: dataset.load_hdf5('/home/nipun/Dropbox/nilmtk_datasets/iawe/')

In [5]: building = dataset.buildings[1]

In [6]: electric = building.utility.electric

In [7]: electric.appliances.keys()

[ApplianceName(name='fridge', instance=1),
 ApplianceName(name='kitchen outlets', instance=1),

Finding the range of voltage for air conditioner 1¶

In [8]: electric.appliances[('air conditioner',1)][('voltage','')].describe()

We observe minimum voltage of 0 and maximum of 5140. Clearly, these are due to some fault in data collection. These readings should be removed

Removing readings in the dataset where voltage >260 or voltage <160¶

In [9]: import nilmtk.preprocessing.electricity.building as prepb

In [10]: building = prepb.filter_out_implausible_values(
   ....:            building, Measurement('voltage', ''), 160, 260)
   ....:

Downsampling the dataset to 1 minute¶

In [13]: building =prepb.downsample(building, rule='1T')

Fill large gaps in appliances with zeros and forward-fill small gaps¶

In [14]: building = prepb.fill_appliance_gaps(building)
